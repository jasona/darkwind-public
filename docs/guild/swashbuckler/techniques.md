Techniques

A young Swashbuckler may use only one technique at a time. With guild
levels, a Swashbuckler will have more *finesse*, and may increase the
depth and number of styles usable at one time.

Using multiple defensive styles together will cause additional strain on
the Swashbuckler, as will multiple offensive styles together.

Note:  You can check what styles you are using by typing *st* or *hp*,
       and you can finish using all styles by typing *stoptech*.

Techniques are either *proc* based, or *round* based. This means the technique either
activates under some condition (when hit, when performing a hit, etc), or once per
round.  The costs happen when the technique is activated, which means a proc-based
technique may activate multiple times per round.

Each triggered technique awards 2 GXP at most once per ten seconds across all
techniques. Flow discounts positive technique costs from 0% at empty to 50%
at full Flow. Zero-cost techniques remain free. While Flow is full, each
eligible combat round also has a 5% chance to steal one guaranteed weapon hit.


## Techniques

### Parry - Guild Level 1

Parry is the simplest of the blade techniques. A parrying
swashbuckler concentrates on deflecting incoming hits that
would otherwise have hit him. Whenever a swashbuckler
successfully parries an incoming attack, the entire force
of the blow will be deflected, doing no damage. Naturally,
the effectiveness of this technique depends on the skill
the swashbuckler has attained in the guild. Of course,
critical hits are hard to parry, catching adventurers
of all types, including swashbucklers, off guard.

SP:  Free (proc)

*Effect(s)*: Chance to deflect damage on-hit


### Slash - Guild Level 2

Slashing can often be an effective, if somewhat crude way
of attacking an opponent. Using brute strength alone, a
swashbuckler attempts to make direct, powerful attacks
against his opponent. While the attacks are no more
likely to hit than usual, blows that land cause greater
damage.

SP:  2 SP (proc)

*Effects(s)*: Chance of +6/+7/+9/+10/+12 damage below Hero and at the Hero,
Legend, Epic, and Mythic brackets.


### Dodge - Guild Level 3

Dodge is a simple technique that allows a swashbuckler to
nimbly dodge incoming attacks. Naturally, the 
effectiveness of this technique depends on the skill and
dexterity of the swashbuckler.

Of course, critical hits are hard to dodge, catching
adventurers of all types, including swashbucklers, off guard.
Attempting to dodge a critical hit like this may still
consume SP.

SP:  5 SP (proc)

*Effects(s)*: Chance to dodge incoming hits


### Offensive Spin - Guild Level 4

The offensive spin is another early blade technique to be
learned. The swashbuckler uses his manual dexterity to spin
his weapons around his body, striking unpredictably. As a
result, his swings become difficult to defend against, and
will land more often upon his opponent.

SP:  3 SP (proc)

*Effects(s)*: 15% increased hit chance


### Calledshot - Guild Level 5

By making a called shot, a swashbuckler can strike at a weak
point in his opponent's armor, or at an unprotected area of
the opponent's body. Achieving this precise aim requires
both a steady hand and knowledge of weaknesses. These well-
placed strikes, when they land, will cause greater damage
to the enemy.

SP:  3 SP (proc)

*Effects(s)*: Chance of +12/+15/+18/+21/+24 damage below Hero and at the Hero,
Legend, Epic, and Mythic brackets.


### Disarm - Guild Level 6

A swashbuckler soon becomes adept at relieving enemies of
their weapons. A successful attempt will cause an opponent
to drop its weapon. There is no effect on targets
using natural weaponry--claws, fists, fangs, etc.

SP:  40 SP (proc)

*Effects(s)*: Chance to disarm opponent


### Riposte - Guild Level 7

Riposte is a mid-level blade technique. The swashbuckler, by
anticipating an attack is able to respond to an incoming
blow from a third party (someone he's not attacking) with a
quick strike of his own. Note that parrying is not necessary
to execute ripostes.

SP:  5 SP (proc)

*Effects(s)*: Chance to redirect an attack from a secondary opponent


### Critical Strikes - Guild Level 8

A skilled swashbuckler can drive their blades deep, 
concentrating on powerful strikes that will result in
large, critical blows. This ability will enhance the
swashbuckler's natural abilities.

SP:  5 SP (proc)

*Effects(s)*: 15% increased critical chance


### Weaponbreak - Guild Level 9

A practised swashbuckler can catch an enemy's weapon on his
blade and, with a sharp twist, break it, rendering the enemy
much weaker. This skill is more intricate and difficult to
learn than disarm, but can also be more effective at
weakening your enemy. Weapons broken in this manner are
considerably reduced in effectiveness

SP:  40 SP (proc)

*Effects(s)*: Chance to break an opponent's weapon


### Phantom Strikes - Guild Level 10

A swashbuckler can call on the ghosts and phantoms of
their fallen brethren. These powerful spirits will attack
with them, lashing out at opponents and haunting their 
defenses.

SP:  6 SP (proc)

*Effects(s)*: Added chaos (plus occasional armour piercing) damage


### Impale - Guild Level 11

Using the impale blade technique, a swashbuckler tries
to inflict deep wounds by following through on each
impaling attack.

SP:  9 SP (proc)

*Effects(s)*: Chance of +45/+56/+67/+78/+90 damage below Hero and at the Hero,
Legend, Epic, and Mythic brackets.


### Flurry - Guild Level 12

Speed is the swashbucklers' greatest advantage in combat,
and nowhere is that more obvious than the flurry skill. By
concentrating on the offensive, a swashbuckler can take
advantage of the slightest openings and strike at his enemy
more often.

Each proc guarantees one extra swing. Hero level unlocks a chance for a
second extra swing, and Legend level unlocks a chance for a third.

SP:  8 SP (proc)

*Effects(s)*: Extra swing(s)


### Flourish - Guild Level 13

The penultimate blade technique, flourish represents the
full realization of a swashbuckler's blade training. The
swashbuckler executing this technique twirls his weapon(s)
in an intricate, beautiful, and deadly series of flourishes.
A swashbuckler executing this technique mesmerizes his
opponent with his skill, enabling the swashbuckler to land
his blows more easily and more powerfully. Rather than
relying on speed, this technique's effectiveness is based
on the swashbuckler's ability to charm and entrance his
opponent.

SP:  6 SP (round)

*Effects(s)*: Improves other techniques as well as abilities


### Thousand Blades - Guild Level 14

This technique provides another way of exploiting your
dexterity, allowing a swashbuckler to spin their blades
in rapid succession, distracting an opponent momentarily.
This distraction will allow more of the swashbuckler's
hits to land.

SP:  5 SP (proc)

*Effects(s)*: 25% increased hit chance


### Dance of Death - Tier 15 passive

The Dance of Death, the kensai's legendary '*whirlwind attack*',
is the swashbuckler's ultimate technique. A
synthesis of mind, body, and blade which is deceptively
delicate and lethally beautiful. It combines the aspects
of many of the swashbuckler's individual techniques to
create a cocoon of steel round the warrior. Dance is now passive: at Tier 15
and 100 Flow, it can very rarely make the next paid triggered technique free.

SP: Free; it discounts one later technique

*Effect*: One-shot free-technique token

### Spectral Siphon - Tier 18

The spectral brethren drink magical strength from an opponent on a successful
hit. A proc costs 6 SP before its Flow discount, drains up to 12 currently
available SP, and restores exactly that amount to the Swashbuckler.

Command: *starttech siphon*

Chapter 2: Talamh Darag, after the Darkwind.

After the Darkwind had blown, causing massive destruction in its wake, only
small groups of the numerous races that had once inhabited Talamh Darag
survived. It appeared a lot of races had gone extinct, including the ancient
race.

With the destruction of the ancient race, the Cinneach Siochair disbanded since
the races no longer shared the same mutual enemy. The surviving races all went 
in their own direction, finding a new place to live and rebuild their society.

The dwarves dug into the ground creating an extensive underground network of 
tunnels to build their homes and harvest natural minerals such as gems. The 
elves created a new home deep in the forest that they named "Baile Gloir", 
"Town of glory". The faeries split off in several small colony's on the 
island and the independent races all went their own way.

Rebuilding of the new civilization was still in progress when elf scouts 
chanced upon a group of adventurous duine, "Humans". They learned the duine 
came from a different island. Sailing huge and massive ships they had explored
the fuathas sàl, "great salt water" or ocean.

The elves and duine, being so much alike, soon saw each other as kindred 
spirits. The duine taught the elves the concept of trade and built a castle 
halfway between their docks and Baile Gloir. This castle functioned as a 
resting place and trading point for the merchants of both races, generating a 
trade route and making for safe travelling. As such it was dubbed Wayfare castle
by the duine.

It didn't take long for the elves to realize the potential in trade and they 
soon established trade routes with the other races. Gnomish ale, dwarven gems
or silky cloth from the faeries - it didn't really matter what one needed, if
it existed one could probably buy it at Baile Gloir.

A glorious and wondrous era for the elves just launched, or so it seemed.

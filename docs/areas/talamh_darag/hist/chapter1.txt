Chapter 1: Talamh Darag, before the Darkwind.

The name "Talamh Darag" stands for "Land of oak trees" in the elder tongue.
Long before the Darkwind blew, Talamh Darag was a land inhabited by three 
factions: the Cinneach Siochair, The ancient race and the Coingeis Gineal.

The Cinneach Siochair, which in the elder tongue meant "Nation of elves, 
faeries and dwarves", was considered the ruling party of Talamh Darag. 
They served the deity Ban-righinn dùrachd which translates to "Queen of 
sincerity and good-will" - the same deity we now refer to as Mitra.

A now presumed extinct race of demonic creatures also lived on Talamh Darag. 
Vile and ghastly in appearance they thrived on causing pain, death and chaos. 
It's probably for this reason that they served Àrd-thainistear an-dòchas, 
"Lord-regent of despair" nowadays better known as Set. The knowledge of 
their true name has been lost over the many centuries, as such they are now 
only known as the "ancient race".

The Coingeis Gineal, which in the elder tongue is closely translated as 
"The independent races" encompassed a broad group of races that were not
particularly aligned to some cause. If they even served a deity it was rarely 
Ban-righinn dùrachd or Àrd-thainistear an-dòchas serving instead Màthair anam, 
"Mother source of life" or the entity we call today, Gaea.

Little is still known about this period of time. With the massive destruction 
caused by the Darkwind most knowledge of this era was forever lost.

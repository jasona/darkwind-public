                              The Test
                              --------

>>>TINK!<<<

The sound of the crossbow bolt hitting the stone wall elicited a
loud curse from the guard whod fired it.  The point of impact had
been exactly three centimeters to the left of the intruders right
ear, seen from the guards perspective.  Said intruder had
recoiled instinctively from the shot, but had recovered a split
second later, and was now advancing -- quickly.  The guard was
reaching for a new bolt, but realized hed never make it.  He
dropped his crossbow and had just enough time to draw his sword
before the intruders quarterstaff sliced the air with a loud
_whoosh_! and connected with the guards head.  Or rather,
connected with the guards _helmet_, which momentarily saved him
from death, but left him with a horrible ringing in his ears, a
distraction which the intruder now exploited, pulling the lead end
of the staff towards him with his right hand whilst simultaneously
pushing the back end of the staff forward with his left --
lightning fast.  The result:  the new end of the staff very rudely
introduced itself to the guards nose.  The impact flattened the
cartilage and shattered the bone underneath, sending fragments
into the brain.  The onset of death was so quick that the guards
face preserved its look of surprise even as the corpse crashed to
the floor.

The next guard was already waiting, had already lined up the shot,
and fired as his colleague fell.  The intruder dodged, his long
blond hair flying, exposing pointed ears.  The bolt skimmed across
the front of his leather body armor, wickedly tearing the fabric,
but not the skin it protected.  The Elf surged forward, swinging
the staff as he came.  But this guard had time to fully draw his
sword and swing.  Staff and sword connected with each other with
a loud  >>>THUNK!<<< The sword bit into the oak -- and got hung
up.  The Elf jerked the staff back, yanking the sword out of the
astonished guards grasp.  The sudden movement loosened the
swords bite and it clattered to the floor.  Before the guard
could react, the Elf swung, connected with the guards face,
reversed the staff, and buried the other end in the solar plexus.
The guard doubled over, suddenly bereft of oxygen, and the Elf
finished him with a mighty downward stroke to the back of the
neck, breaking it.

But now more guards were racing toward him from down the hall as
the alarm spread.  The Elf turned and ran back to the room where
hed despatched the first crossbowman.  There he found the
opposite doorway -- his escape route -- blocked by another guard,
newly arrived.  Without hesitation, the Elf pounced and attacked
furiously.  Exactly three seconds later, the man was down.  But
now the guards from the hallway -- four of them -- had gained the
room, and the Elf fled through the doorway just before they could
surround him and just as another crossbow bolt struck the archway
above the Elfs head.

Down the hall, a right turn and >>>SMACK!<<< into a guard who was
just around the corner.  Elf and guard both went down on their
respective arses, but the Elf regained his feet faster, and one
jab to the gut later, the Elf was racing down the hallway, leaving
the guard doubled over, alive only because the Elfs time had
grown short -- "escape" had become the prime directive.

At the next intersection, a left, but no!  The hallway was filled
with guards, so the Elf doubled back as a crossbow bolt zoomed
past his ear.  But now the hallway from which he had come was also
swarming with guards, which left him one choice -- the door to his
right, which led to the courtyard, the one place where he knew he
should not go, and now the only place he _could_ go.

The door was locked.

>>>BASH! SPLINTER!<<<

Correction -- the door was UN-locked.

The delay allowed the guards to close up, and as the Elf burst
into the courtyard, he felt something stick him in the lower back.
The pain came a moment later, and he smelled blood -- his own.
Despite the pain, the Elf whirled about and >>>KLANG!<<< a guard
fell to his knees, holding his head, sudden victim of a
particularly wicked migraine.  Now more guards were boiling out
of doorways around the courtyard.  In the dim light, the Elf
sensed exactly eight of them.  They had him surrounded on three
sides and were closing the noose all the way.  Again the Elf
whirled, swung the staff in a wide arc, the exertion further
aggravating the wound and making him grimace in pain.  The staff
failed to connect, but it was enough to make the guards back off,
buying the Elf one precious second to keep his back free and take
in his surroundings -- no exit, no place to climb, and now more
guards appearing in the upper windows, bolting their crossbows.

The guards knew they had him.  They advanced at a leisurely pace
now, grinning evilly, taunting him.

"Little men with pointed ears!" a guard sneered.

"We will _trim_ them for you!" promised another, and they all
laughed.

"Out of the way, let me through!" bellowed another, a rather
large man who now pushed his way to the front -- the Captain,
judging by the insignia on his shoulders.  He smirked at the Elf.
"Great warrior you may be," he said, "but against so many
swords--" he waved his hand around to indicate his men --"I fear
the battle would be short-lived."

The Elf simply crouched, staff held at the ready, eyes boring into
the Captain, blood trickling down his leg from the wound in his
back.

"Last chance, Elf -- put the staff _down_!  Its over!"

Eight armed men stood before him with drawn swords.  Another three
were in the windows around the courtyard, crossbows ready.  The
Elf made a decision.

He sighed, relaxed, and lowered the staff.  The Captain grinned.
Suddenly the Elf tensed again and threw the staff toward the
guards.  As they instinctively recoiled in surprise, all eyes on
the flying staff, the Elf turned and dove >>>KERPLOOSH!<<< into
the large water trough located on this side of the courtyard.

The Captain caught the staff, looked at it briefly, and then threw
it away.  His men were all looking at him.  "Well?!"  he shouted.
The guards snapped out of it and quickly moved to the trough and
surrounded it.  The night had grown silent now, save for the
random splashing and rippling of the water into which the Elf had
dove.

"Caint old is breath ferever, can e?" said one, and they all
chuckled.

"And he aint got his nasty little stick anymore, does he now?"
offered the guard with the "migraine", still clutching his head.

And so the guards stood around the water trough, drawn swords at
the ready, waiting.

What happened next was so sudden and violent, that no two men --
those that _survived_, that is -- would later give the same
version of it.

Something exploded out of the water, knocked one of the guards on
his back, leapt to another guard, knocked him down too, and then
attacked the Captain.  The two guards on the ground were quite
still.  Closer inspection would reveal that each of them had a
bloody, pulpy mess where their throats should have been.

The Captain was screaming and thrashing around wildly.  The
Creature from the Water Trough had attached itself to his right
hand, the one that held the sword.  One sickening, fleshy, ripping
sound later, the sword clattered to the ground as the hand
separated from the arm.

Now for the first time, the thing stood still.  It was a wolf, a
big grey one.  In its mouth was what remained of the Captains
right hand.  The Wolf bit down on it once and swallowed, licked
its bloody chops, opened its mouth, and _grinned_.  The guards
just stood there, jaws hanging open.  The Captain clutched at his
right arm, groaning and trying to staunch the flow of blood.

Then, instinct overcame good sense, and the Wolf involuntarily did
what all animals do when theyre soaking wet -- he shook himself.
_Wet.__Get_dry_.

The delay was enough for the guards to recover from their shock,
assess the situation, and react accordingly -- by bravely running
away.  The Wolf did not pursue.  After leaping from the pool,
lunging at two guards, and maiming the Captain -- all of it while
wounded -- it was spent.  _Pain_.  Its hind legs suddenly buckled.
The Wolf turned its head around to lick the wound on its lower
back.  _Hunger_!   The fresh blood of the two corpses beckoned
irresistibly.  Using its forelegs, the Wolf pulled itself over to
the nearest dead guard, opened its jaw to reveal the razor-sharp
teeth within, and plunged its snout into the corpses chest.
_Hunger_!  The flesh and bone gave way easily, and in no time,
the Wolf had the heart in its teeth.  And then in its stomach.
Next came the liver.  And then some meat off the ribs -- nothing
like the meat thats closest to the bone!  _Yummy_!

Some feeling was returning to the hind legs.  The wolf hacked up
a hairball and went to the second corpse.  Heart, liver, ribs.
Uncontrollable slobber.  Snout soaked in blood.  _Ecstasy_!

The Wolf was quite enjoying itself until something thudded into
its left shoulder.  The pain came a second later.  _Hurt_.  From
a second story window, a guard waved his crossbow high in the air
and whooped, "I got im!  I got im!  Damned, blasted mutt!  How
ya like that hot love up your arse, doggy, woof woof!
Hahahahahaha!"  Other crossbowmen were taking up positions.
_Danger_!  A sudden instinctive urge caused the wolf to jump to
his left, right out of the path of a swinging sword; the Captain
-- now fighting left-handed.  _Revenge_!  Thhe Wolf took him by the
throat and shook.  >>>TINK!<<<  A bolt bounced harmlessly off the
stone floor to the wolfs right.  >>>THWOK!<<< Another one hit the
captain, as if the wolf needed help, and a crossbowman cursed from
above.

_Dead_.  The Wolf released the captain and dug with its teeth for
the heart, ripped it out of the corpses chest, bit down twice,
and swallowed.  Good!  The wolf knew instinctively that it didnt
have time for this, but it also knew that it would never escape
from this Bad Situation while wounded if it didnt have some
extra energy.

>>>TINK! TINK! TINK!<<< The wolfs luck held as three more bolts
missed, striking the floor around him.  _Flee_!  The wolf moved,
covering the distance to the door in one fourth of the time the
Elf needed, sped along the hallway to the exit, flattened another
guard along the way just for good measure -- and was free, flying
away from the keep into the safety of the deep woods...

 ----------

...and coming finally to the rendezvous point.  The Wolf
collapsed, breathing hard from the exertion and its wounds.
_Pain_.__Fatigue_.  The wolf lay on its right side, the bolt still
in its left shoulder.

_Coming_.  The wolf sensed the humanoids approach even before the
scent reached its nose.  Then he smelled it.  Leader.

A kind of singing in his head ... _you_need_food_.__Eat_.  The man
dropped the carcass of a deer down on the ground next to the Wolf.
The wolfs salivary glands went wild, and he feasted for some
time.  The man waited patiently while the wolf took the corpse
apart, consumed all the meat it could find, licked the bones.

_Sated_.

The man nodded, reached over, and yanked out the bolt.  The wolf
gave a short yelp and sighed.

_How_do_you_feel_?

_Feel_.__Fine_.

_Man,_now_.__And_then_rest_.

The Wolf closed its eyes.  Its fur began to thin and disappear.
It gave a little whine and passed out.

----------

The Elf awoke and sensed that it was just before dawn.  He opened
his eyes and saw the man, sitting cross-legged on the grass,
watching him with a little smile.

The Elf sat up slowly, giving the muscles time to adjust.  Then he
made eye contact, bowed his head slightly and said, "Pack Leader."

The man inclined his head slightly forward.  "Pup".  His smile
widened.

The Elf dropped his gaze and sighed.  "I have failed."

Leader smiled benignly.  "What was your task?"

"To retrieve the topaz from the treasure room and bring it here.
But I -- could not reach it."

"Tell me."

The Elf related the events from his penetration of the Keep up
until his dive into the water trough.  And then he was silent.

Leader simply waited.  The Elf looked ashamed.

"Continue," Leader said.

The Elf could not meet his gaze.

Leader sighed.  "What restriction did I place upon you for this
task?"

Finally the Elf found his voice.  "I was to -- fight only on two
legs."

Leader waited.

"I -- I entered the water.  But it was the Wolf that came out."

Leader nodded.  "And why did you violate my restriction?"

The Elf swallowed.  Then he found the courage to make eye contact.
"Forgive me, my Leader.  The alternative -- was death."

Leader just looked at him.  Summoning all his courage, the Elf
held the gaze.  Finally, after what seemed to the Elf like an
eternity, his Leader nodded and smiled.  "You did fine, Pup.  You
did fine."

The Elf blinked.  This was the last thing he expected to hear.

"I feel I owe you an apology," continued Leader.  "There was no
topaz in the treasure room.  And at your current level of skill,
fighting on only two legs, it would have taken nothing short of
divine intervention for you to even reach the treasure chamber,
let alone escape alive."

The Elfs jaw fell slightly open.  He gave a little shake of his
head.  "Then ... why?"

Leader smiled slightly.  "Your true task this night was to face
an impossible situation -- and master it.  Even if it meant
disobeying your Pack Leader."

Leader stood up.  The Elf did likewise.  Leader smiled and laid
both hands on the Elfs shoulders.  "You did fine, Pup.  You did
_real_ fine!"

Leader removed his hands and dropped to one knee, staring into the
Elfs eyes.  He then raised his right arm, slowly formed a fist,
and bowed his head before the Elf.

The honor stunned him.  Then he remembered himself and repeated
the rite to his Leader.

Leader smiled and clapped the Elf on the back.  "Come on, Pup --
lets go home.  Been a long night!"

And as dawn lit up the world, the two figures faded into the
woods.

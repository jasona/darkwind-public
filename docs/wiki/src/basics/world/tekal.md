# Tekal

Work in progress: this page describes the Tekal setting we are building toward,
and current game content differs in places.

Tekal is the green moon of knowledge, ash-electric steam technology, sealed
archives, Vyra refinement, and forbidden augmentation. It is the technological
step beyond Geshtei's medieval world, but it is not a gunpowder or laser
future. Tekal's wonders are made from ash-fired furnaces, pressure engines,
copper conduits, green glass, calculation halls, Vyrariums, academies,
observatories, and locked doors.

Later Tekal expansions may push farther into robotics, quantum computing,
virtual reality, and official cybernetics labs. The first Tekal arc should
establish the ash-electric foundation before those higher-tech layers become
playable.

Tekal's tagline:

> The light of knowledge casts the deepest shadows.

The Tekai believe knowledge is sacred, dangerous, and earned through
discipline. Surface Tekal is beautiful and brilliant, but also class-bound and
secretive. The ruling Knowledge Houses decide who may learn, who may heal, who
may build, and who must remain outside the next gate.

## Core Concepts

- Ash-electricity powers Tekal through ash-fired steam systems, turbines,
  coils, lamps, gates, and pressure infrastructure.
- Miniaturized ash-electric ranged weapons are unstable and banned after the
  Small Coil Catastrophe. Tekal has no guns, no lasers, and no ranged
  ash-weapons.
- Vyra is the root liquid refined into the Core Four.
- Vyrariums are shops or clinics that sell, refine, serve, or administer Vyra
  and its variants.
- Nethrys Undercity uses banned cybernetics, implants, and Vyra-enhanced
  augmentation beneath the surface.

## Nethrys And Reca

Nethrys is not a small sewer district. It is a large underground city beneath
Tekal, full of black-market Vyrariums, forbidden schools, smugglers, worker
refuges, ripperdoc implant clinics, and banned cybernetic technology.

Its mastermind is Reca, Ringleader of the Green Door Society. Reca is part man
and part machine, a charismatic and ruthless visionary who believes Tekal's
future lies in unrestricted technological evolution. He opposes the Knowledge
Houses, the Council, and the surface belief that knowledge must stay locked
away.

## What Players Find Here

- Green-glass cities, sealed archives, pressure gates, and ash-electric
  machinery.
- Licensed Vyrariums on the surface and black-market Vyrariums below.
- Knowledge ranks, credentials, scholar-guards, and controlled civic access.
- Industrial ash hazards, greenhouse experiments, academy trials, and celestial
  moon-lore.
- Banned implants and cybernetics in Nethrys. The Forbidden Replacement Table
  in the Ripperdoc Quarter is a staffed clinic for implant installation and
  extraction.
- The truth of the Small Coil Catastrophe.

## Travel Mood

Tekal feels precise, beautiful, and judgmental. Doors open only for the
credentialed. Lamps glow behind green glass. Machines breathe steam beneath
stone. Scholars speak softly while hiding entire histories.

The danger is not wilderness. The danger is a society that understands systems
better than mercy, and knowledge better than freedom.
